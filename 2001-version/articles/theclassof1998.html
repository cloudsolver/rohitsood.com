The Class of 1998
       
As the new millennium unfolds itself offering myriad possibilities and puzzles, I stop to think: why is a period of 1000 years called ‘millennium’? Do people still respect engineers and would-be-engineers at the fag end of the 20th century? Or should they? Find out. ..did I mention 'fag'?
Well that is the source of sustenance to the MIET engineer. For the extra-broad minded folks, we are talking in terms of tobacco and not what you’re thinking we’re talking about. Block No.5 is jam-packed with the technocratic species, and it oozes out with technical spunk and other quite unprintable stuff that would make any outsider gape in apparent disbelief. But we are like this only, mind it!
TECHIE VILLAGE
Achtung/Warning: I do not want to vaunt tenets of impartial journalism that’s because this literary work is totally biased, and is seen from "my-eye-view". Anybody who takes this seriously is stupid. Period. Block No.5 is the center of the campus (just like the Earth is the center of the Milky Way and the heart is the center of the human body). At the heart of the universe, at the border between the light and the dark stands block No.5 inside which abode humans who defy description. But we shall attempt to jump in headfirst into the wild side of college life in central India, so read all about it ahead.
The nondescript village, popularly known as United Kudwa is proud to have a ‘fine’ institute. This is where the students are fined for almost everything, if no reason can be found to enforce a monetary penalty then a general fine is imposed. This is the place where the shit hits the road before the Sun hits the sky. It appears (actually it stinks) that the Kudwaites can’t tell the difference between a road and a commode. THE TECHIES
It’s early in the sultry morning; I scurry off to room No.5001. pen and pad in hand. Its blindingly bright in here, I picture the silhouette of a large man on the small, cemented center bed against the blaze of the Sun (at 10:55hrs early in the morning), flooding in through the open windows 98 (in the year of our lord: Bill Gates). This nocturnal creature is often found in a state of suspended animation in his very own private incoherent psychedelic world. Mohit Palhan is of Titanic proportions and predictably his profile (academic & otherwise) is far below the C-level. He is unquestionably the most well read guy around. Very religious and logical; though a paradoxical combination, it suits him just fine.
THE COMPUTER AND ROCK'N'ROLL
In the room opposite, rests a Pentium with MMX (More Masti Xclusive) and an Intel Inside and a cretin outside clicking at the poor vulnerable machine like a loony Zulu beating the war drums while sitting on an itchy rock. Earlier in the year our Rock’n’roll man was involved in an exhibition of eggs from chicks and robotic moving fingers and other such gizmos.
Shantanu a.k.a. mouth-organ man has frustrated neighbors who are of the likes of Anand Singh, the sorry-wannabe, Sushant ‘Who-You’ Ghosh and Ashish ‘I’m straight’ Rai. Andy is the studious kind, any blind man can say that, but what characterizes his personality is his nauseating sweetness
STICKLERS FOR PROTOCOL
Ghosh and Rai don’t deviate too much from each other but are sticklers for protocol, none-the-less. I’m parsimonious when it comes to flattery, but Sandhu is my fave Sardarji (not counting Kushwant Singh who is my all time favorite), he lives only a few rooms down. He’s known to perpetuate the entire gamut of whimsical fundas varying from the great metaphysical profundities of "space & time" (the final frontier), to his very own life history which is hilarious. If you’ve got a problem .Yo! He’ll solve it; check out the hood while the surdy revolves it!
Ice-ice babies J.P.Singh and Naliwal aren’t to be left far behind, and they too enjoy the last year of B.E. to the limit and beyond. Next room is where H.V.Sikravar is never found. He’s mostly on his bike, the one with the Hitler’s symbol on it. Nobody can quite relate the Swastika and Sikravar nexus. He’s more gauche than one could imagine; tries hard to be the macho-kind; surely he needs a paradigm of masculinity to emulate in order to obscure the subtle femininity which is far more conspicuous than his poor academic record and 'netagiri'.
BIHARI ROOM
The Bihari room is constantly buzzing with academic activities. The air is heavy with incomprehensible technical jargons and sandalwood incense. Rupesh, Nand and Hemant are almost always found glued to their chairs and desk, doing what everybody should be doing: studying.
I slip out of the room before they realize. But guess whom I bumped into as I turned around? Aishwarya Rai? Sushmita Sen.? Diana Hayden? No way dudes it is Mr. Mohit Sood (from Ranchi), only his 350cc Yezdi matches his personality, a champion heavyweight lifter and a prospective WWF wrestler. Today he’s carrying textbooks (the weight of 8085 is more than one can imagine).
His 'roomy' (read: Room mate) is cool personified; calm about any and everything, Mr. Arnab Ganguly (from Bangalore) is one of the most soft-spoken persons seen by the Hostels of M.I.E.T in several years. Their neighbors are Saunak Mallik, Anirrudha Bagchi and Vinod Menon. The first two are from Calcutta and the insufferable third is sleeping.
BENGALI DUO
The Bengali duo is smarter than what one might conceive them to be. Along with the Bihari group they form the intellectual epicenter of the5th block. Saunak’s the sly-by-fly nincompoop while Bagchi is very helpful when it comes to academics. These two in a million actually work. And hard, too! As I take my leave from their room with my nose blocked, head cloudy, eyes teary-red and face burning I realize I hate sandalwood incense as much as I hate pizzas with chocolate sauce or vanilla ice-cream with tomato ketchup.
Why don’t the ‘aggarbatti’ (incense) manufacturers invent new odors for a change? I wonder where the rest of the electronic guys are? I’ll check upstairs, the penthouse, the top-floor, the floor where my room is; the first floor. On the second flight of the fifth block in the last decade of the 20th century I encounter two smooth musketeers who sport moustaches that would be the object of envy even for the Maharajas of the 12th century. Sanjay Pratap Singh alias S.P. sir / bhaiya and Pradip Sharma (from the land of Bhopalis; Bhopal, M.P.). The former is often seen in the football-cum-volleyball –cum-walking -- cum-cricket ground on our campus in his trademarked red overalls that adds a new color this otherwise colorful personality. Never have I seen him troubled, distraught or angry (thank heavens for small mercies), he is believed to be the epitome of genuinity.
The latter, by far, is not too different ... Sharma too is kind and generous. As the Englishman would put it -"Jolly good chaps!" Following close behind and descending the flight of stairs is Amit Rao, who like S.P.Singh is quite a sportsman himself. Rao has a special dislike for people who talk too much; in his own words he doesn’t like those who indulge in excess ‘bakkar’. (I thought he liked himself!) . His agnostic aptitude eclipses his otherwise irreverent activities. (That's for me to know and you to find out.) and sometimes his arch rival and namesake.
THE FIRST FLOOR
I am now on the first floor balcony (the one that faces the Bank, co-op store, PCO, the ashram, and, the temples). This quarter is the home for Madhu "nipple sucker" Joshi , Naveen "I love to solder" Pahwa , Om "bathroom singer" Bajpai , Kamal "eunuch voice" Jain and Abhishek "yeh gashti hai" Sharma . Madhu as the name suggests is all honey , sweet like a sugar cane ; he plays tennis now and then , but maintains a surprisingly low profile .
Our professional camera-man , Naveen has a semi-automatic with which he shoots pictures that would make a buffalo blush , my what a waste of precious film .To be honest, this energetic guy is really good at it . Jain on the other hand is particularly good at poking his running nose in other people’s business besides his infamous nocturnal habit of singing in his despicable nasal voice which would send shivers up a ghost’s spine, then again he pours out his abhorrent collection of sher and shayari . Om ,I hear is scared of ghosts and it is said that when nature calls on him late in the boogie-nights he visits the loo singing off-tune chants to ward off the evil spirits.
Abhishek Sharma (hails from the land of five rivers), he has his roots deep in Africa, Libya (no joke!) and is currently earning merits at Kudwa and our mega-city Gondia . He’s a dynamic sportsman and maintains an obscenely large group of friends and acquaintances ,quite like Mohit Pallhan in the P.R. department . He is characterized by his strong gynophobia , but after four years of constant exposure , the fear of the opposite gender has relaxed along with the libido.
Rohit Gupta is our chewy-moue , who always is munching and drooling all day long , at night he transforms into a disco-zombie , after 11pmyou’ll find him stoned out of his senses listening to music, which is far behind the prescribed decibel-level and far beyond comprehension . But ,come study time and his best friends are the books and he dives in headfirst, which explains his academic prowess.
OTHER ENTITIES
He is not your regular next-door-neighbor-nice-guy-types ,looks can be deceiving. On the other hand we have Amit Bhatnagar who by contrast is kind and gentle ,he might just make it big time on MTV or Channel V , a gem of a person . The same can’t be said for Ravi Panda but he’s soft spoken too, Chandan Majumdar is an interesting guy almost as interesting as a dead tortoise ,and just to top that he is also is too obstreperous, rock rigid and mentally singular to be bothered with .
Who can forget Sachin Chowdhry , who had crushes quicker than a toad gulps flies , this kiddish and innocent guy is quite popular with the big daddies of Gondia City? He is jerkish but passable as a quality gem . I now walk toward room no.5116 , my neighbors are Arun "Big’n’Black"Pandey , Mansoor "Kinetic" Ali , Amit "Bhau" Atre , Somesh "Pseudo-macho" Sanghi , and Paritosh " I fram Dongarhgarh" Basant . Pleasant guys these , and most of the time they keep to themselves which I appreciate a lot.
MY ROOM-MATES AND I
My roommates and I vehemently repulse most of the people ; we impetuously concoct the exoneration of our dyspeptic brains to impoverish those who dare to darken our doorstep . It can’t be helped , 'we are like this only , mind it'.
When we’re good we’re very good , but when we’re bad we’re better . My roommates are in two models a white sardar and a paunchy bong. It would take another epic to describe these eccentric idiosyncratic frugalities of nature's cruel joke on mankind. Sure ,there are many students who are not unfortunate enough to stay in the hostel with us , Amit Chatterjee is one , and he tops the class with uncanny persistence . Ashish Awasthi is one of those guys who knows a little of everything , he’s especially vociferous on the poetic side.
THE LADIES
Zeena Chakraborthy and Kuhu Bhattacharya bring a freshness to the class- it's good to see non-male faces once in a while. They represent the intellectual and physical highs and lows of the class . Zeena is our lean-mean-educational-machine whose promiscuous activities are far more interesting than her otherwise conspicuous academic faux pas and irreverent pardons associated ; on the other hand , Kuhu’s sweetness is only matched by honeydew and sugar-coated velvet voice , good things do come in small packages . The other female engineers of the likes of Usha " Tamil Brain" Kumari , Sapna "Indira Gandhi cut" Gupta , Hema "Gimme food" Manuja , Sarita "Mew" Kesarwani , Neerja "Blood for Blood" Narla , and Breethi "Fangs" Banerjee make up the class which is always filled with fun and excitement. Well ,that more or less , covers our class : the students of final year electronics , the class of 1998
As the last few months turn into the last few weeks ,then days , a time will come to say the goodbyes and the best-of-lucks .But , what will happen to the institute ? What will happen to our favorite chair at Relish ? The terrace of La-Cartel’s ? The drive to Jain Resort ? The friends and foes we made here ? What will happen to sky and the heavens ? Oh well ! I don’t wish to prevaricate metaphysical profundities but basically life must move on , and it does . So as we do good and bad things, we come to realize it is better to do good (although bitching is acceptable and enjoyable). Therefore the moral of this story is : ‘I shall pass this way only once so any good that I may do let me do it now , for I shall never pass this way again."
-Rohit Sood (April-May 1998)
